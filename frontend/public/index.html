Risk Factors

Temperature Extremes: Temperatures below 10°C (50°F) or above 35°C (95°F) can cause blossom drop and reduce fruit set.
Disease Pressure: High humidity increases risk of fungal diseases like early blight and late blight.
Pest Challenges: Tomato hornworm, aphids, and whiteflies can reduce yields if not managed.

Recommendations

Variety Selection: Consider heat-tolerant varieties like 'Sun Gold', 'Celebrity', or 'Roma' for your climate zone.
Planting Strategy: Start seeds indoors 6-8 weeks before outdoor planting date for stronger transplants.
Spacing: Plant 40-60 cm apart for determinate varieties, 60-90 cm for indeterminate varieties.
Succession Planting: Plant a second crop in February for extended harvest season.
